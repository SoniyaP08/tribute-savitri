Savitri : The Legend like None other

Savitri Ganesan was an Indian actress, playback singer, dancer, director, and producer known for her works primarily in Tamil and Telugu cinema. She had also worked in Kannada, Hindi and Malayalam films. She starred in more than 250 films over three decades. She was one of the highest-paid and most popular Indian actresses from the 1950s to early 70s, and is known by the epithets Mahanati and Nadigaiyar Thilagam. She is one of the most accomplished, most versatile and she is one of the most respected actresses in SIFI. She is known for her liveliness and her captivating performance and is still considered as the benchmark for acting in South Indian movies.

Savitri acted in dance dramas as a child, including some work with a theatre company run by Jaggayya. She made an unsuccessful speculative trip to find film work in Madras at the age of 14 when she was deemed to be too young to play heroine roles, but in 1950 was cast as the female lead in Samsaram.

Her performance in the 1957 film Mayabazar skyrocketed her to stardom. She later went on to become the highest-paid and most sought-after South-Indian actress of her generation.

In 1960, she received the Rashtrapati Award which later became "National Award for the Best Actress(1)" for her performance in the Telugu film Chivaraku Migiledi.

Savitri died on 26 December 1981, at the age of 47, after being in a coma for 19 months. She had developed diabetes and high blood pressure.

Awards:

Rashtrapati Award for Chivaraku Migiledi in 1960, Nandi Award for Chinnari Papalu in 1968 and many Filmfare(south) Awards.
